Autonomous Navigation Aerial Vehicle

An advanced drone engineered for intelligent navigation and safe landing in complex environments. This project by Team Njord from IIIT Dharwad showcases a robust system for autonomous scanning, perception, and mission execution.

Explore the Design See it in Action

Mission Objective

The core mission is to develop an ANAV capable of autonomously scanning a designated arena, identifying all potential safe landing spots based on surface characteristics, and then sequentially landing on and taking off from each ranked spot. The mission is complete once all spots have been visited, demonstrating a full cycle of perception, decision-making, and precision control in a GPS-denied environment.

System Architecture

The ANAV's functionality is built upon a sophisticated architecture of interconnected systems. This section breaks down the core components into three main categories: Perception & Sensing, Computation & Control, and Propulsion & Power. Use the tabs below to explore how each system contributes to the drone's autonomous operation.

Intel RealSense D455

Primary Perception Sensor

Provides synchronized RGB and depth data, crucial for 3D environment mapping, obstacle avoidance, and safe spot detection.

CS20 Solid State LiDAR

Ranging Sensor

A high-frequency LiDAR used for accurate altitude hold and terrain following, enhancing the reliability of depth perception.

Optical Flow Sensor

Velocity Estimation

Measures ground velocity, enabling stable flight and accurate position holding in GPS-denied environments.

Inertial Measurement Unit (IMU)

Flight Control Sensor

Combines accelerometers and gyroscopes to measure the drone's orientation and angular rate, fundamental for flight stability.

Barometer

Altitude Sensor

Measures atmospheric pressure to provide stable altitude estimation, complementing other height sensors.

NVIDIA Jetson Xavier NX

Flight Control Computer

The central processing unit that runs high-level tasks like perception algorithms, path planning, and mission logic.

Cube Orange+ Flight Controller

Flight Management System

Manages low-level flight control, executing commands from the main computer and ensuring stable flight through its integrated IMUs and sensor suite.

Holybro Sik Telemetry Radio

Communication Link

Provides a reliable data link between the ANAV and the Ground Control Station (GCS) for real-time monitoring and control.

T-Motor F1000

Motors

High-efficiency brushless motors selected for their thrust-to-weight ratio and reliability, providing the power needed for agile flight.

28-inch Propellers

Propellers

Large propellers matched with the motors to maximize efficiency and provide stable lift for the ANAV's payload.

6S LiPo Battery

Power Source

A high-capacity Lithium Polymer battery provides the necessary power for all onboard systems and a sufficient flight time to complete the mission.

Autonomous Capabilities

The true innovation of the ANAV lies in its software and autonomous decision-making processes. This section outlines the core navigation framework and the step-by-step logic the drone follows to successfully complete its mission, from takeoff to final disarm.

Navigation Framework

The ANAV operates on the NED (North-East-Down) coordinate system. This is a standard local tangent plane reference frame used in aerospace:

  • X-axis: Points to geographic North.
  • Y-axis: Points to geographic East.
  • Z-axis: Points Down towards the Earth's center.

Adopting the NED frame simplifies calculations for navigation, velocity, and altitude control, ensuring consistent and reliable interpretation of data from onboard sensors like the IMU and barometer.

Mission Execution Flow

1. Takeoff & Scan Mode

ANAV performs a stable vertical takeoff and ascends to a scanning height of 3.5 meters. It then patrols the arena, keeping within boundaries, while its perception system actively identifies potential safe landing spots.

2. Ranking & Selection

Once scanning is complete, an onboard algorithm ranks the detected spots from most to least suitable based on size, flatness, and other criteria. It automatically selects the highest-ranked spot for the first landing.

3. Sequential Landing

The ANAV autonomously navigates to and lands precisely on the selected safe spot. After a brief pause, it takes off again and proceeds to the next spot in the ranked list, repeating the process.

4. Mission Accomplished

After landing on the final safe spot, the ANAV declares the mission accomplished via the telemetry link and automatically disarms its motors, concluding the autonomous operation.
